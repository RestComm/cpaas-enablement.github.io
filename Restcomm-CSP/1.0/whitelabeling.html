White Labeling

1. Overview

Restcomm lets you go to market with your own brand by providing a set of white-labeling features within its console.

2. Whitelabeling your Restcomm organizations

Restcomm can be white-labeled using the white-labeling option under your account settings. The white-labeling feature lets you set your company name, logo, favicon, support and documentation URLs, terms, your custom domain, and styling.

2.1. General Organization settings

To begin the white-labeling process, log into your Restcomm account, navigate to Account → Settings → Whitelabeling.

In the General Organization settings form:

  • Enter the name of your organization

  • Upload your company logo (with minimum required size of 75 x 75 px).

    Your company logo will be displayed on the top left corner of the Console, Visual Designer and WebRTC demo user interfaces.

  • Upload your favicon (with required size of 16 x 16 px or 32 x 32 px)

  • Specify your support URL (optional).

    Provide a way for your users to reach out to you in case they want to report issues or ask questions. Link your support page or provide an email address.

  • Provide a link to your documentation (optional).

    Make it easier for your users to get started by sharing your documentation page with them.

  • Add a link to your terms.

    Let users know what your terms and conditions are and how they can utilize your services by linking them here.

2.2. Setting up your custom domain

You can skip this step if you do not want to use a custom domain. Otherwise, follow the steps below.

In order to use your custom domain with Restcomm, you will need to:

  • Provide a valid certificate obtained by trusted third party organization (like GoDaddy)

    • You should provide a full chain certificate in PEM format, that obtains Root CA and intermediate CAs

Setting up your SSL certificate with GoDaddy

To start using white labeling, you must first set up an SSL Certificate for the sub-domain and upload full chain of certificate on Restcomm via Swagger UI. Make sure the order of the SSL Certificate chain is as follows; your domain certificate, your private key, authority issuing your certificate, root certificate.

For simplicity let’s assume your domain is testxmpp.com and for UC-One you are using the following sub-domain: text.testxmpp.com. In the steps below we will explain how to generate a private key and certificate using OpenSSL.

  1. Generate an openssl key (GSS bought a domain on Godaddy text.textxmpp.com) openssl genrsa -out text.testxmpp.com.key 2048

  2. Create your CSR or certification request file

    $ openssl req -new -key text.testxmpp.com.key -out text.testxmpp.com.csr

  3. Copy the content of the newly created text.testxmpp.com.csr and paste it into the Godaddy CSR enrollment form.

  4. When you get the confirmation, download the certificate zip file from you Godaddy Dashboard

  5. The content of the certificate zip file for Tomcat should look like the one below

  6. Combine the certificate from Godaddy with the openssl key you generated in step 1:

    $ cat '/home/charles/Downloads/text.testxmpp.com/51e1fbfeeae30fdb.crt' '/home/charles/telestax/text.testxmpp.com.key' '/home/charles/Downloads/text.testxmpp.com/gd_bundle-g2-g1.crt' > text.testxmpp.combined.pem

  7. The newly created text.testxmpp.combined.pem will contain the certificate you need to create a new domain mapping in swagger. Copy and paste the content of the combine.pem to swagger UI.

  8. If the mapping and the certificate is valid, you will see the following response

  9. Create DNS SRV record pointing to Restcomm Message Exchange. Your DNS SRV record should look like the one below:

    _xmpp-server._tcp.xmpp.mycompany.com. 1 1 5269 cloud-xmpp-server.restcomm.com.

2.3. Setting up your custom domain

The custom styling sections let you apply your styling to the Restcomm Console, Visual Designer, and WebRTC demo.

Here are the styling options you may use:

  • Setting up a background image (preferably in PNG format with minimum size of XXXpx)

  • Basic text color

  • Links color

  • Links color on mouse over

  • Buttons background color

  • Buttons background color on mouse over

  • Buttons text color

  • Buttons text color on mouse over

  • Background color of the Console’s home page

  • Text color of discrete elements

If you wish, you can apply the same styling to the Visual Designer and WebRTC demo by checking the box next to each one of the options.

If you want to set custom styling for the Visual Designer and WebRTC Demo, just leave the boxes unchecked and set the following styling options for each one of them.

Once done, click Submit to apply your changes.
