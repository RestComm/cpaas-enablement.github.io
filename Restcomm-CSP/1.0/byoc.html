Bring Your Own Carrier

1. Overview

1.1. Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC) Summary

Restcomm’s bring your own carrier (BYOC) feature lets Communication Service Providers (CSP) connect to the platform and allows their enterprise customers to use their network by default for PSTN traffic.

BYOC enables CSPs to layer the value of a modern CPaaS on top of their existing PSTN investment.

2. Configuring your own carrier

2.1. Setting up your carrier for voice traffic

Voice origination

Restcomm’s Programmable Voice enables you to initiate SIP sessions from your existing PSTN Carrier (via SIP SBC) towards Restcomm Cloud and use RCML and/or the REST APIs to create advanced voice applications.

When you sign up for a Restcomm Cloud account, you are assigned a Restcomm Organization which provides a custom DNS hostname, in the form of <organization_name>.restcomm.com, that can accept SIP traffic from your network.

At the moment to enable your custom Domain to receive SIP Voice Traffic from your carrier, you need to make sure your equipment can resolve Restcomm DNS SRV Records for your organization to have the interconnection working properly. Your carrier will need to resolve the provided hostname as explained in RFC 3263.

To be able to receive SIP registration and voice traffic from SIP or WebRTC Endpoints, you will need to configure Restcomm SIP Clients with a username and password that should be used on the SIP Client for SIP Digest Authentication

No special configuration is needed to enable your custom domain to receive SIP voice traffic from your carrier.

Voice termination

Restcomm’s Programmable Voice enables your applications to initiate SIP sessions from Restcomm towards your existing PSTN Carrier or SIP/WebRTC communications infrastructure using RCML and/or the REST APIs.

There are multiple ways in Restcomm to route termination traffic using the Dial Verb:

  • Making calls to SIP/WebRTC or other carriers using Dial SIP; The <Sip> noun specifies a SIP URI to dial. This doesn’t require any settings change in order to connect to another SIP infrastructure,

  • Making calls to the default PSTN carrier configured for your account using the following Restcomm capabilities.

    • Dial Number should be used in Restcomm Visual Designer (RVD) or RCML

    • WebRTC demo can initiate a call to a PSTN Number directly

    • HTTP Call via the Call API

Currently the Voice Origination and Phone Number Providers are tied together so if you configure the Voice Origination Providers, you won’t be able to buy Provider Numbers from Restcomm.
To setup Voice Termination with your own carrier for your Organization, please follow the steps below:

  • Log into your Restcomm cloud account and navigate to your profile → Settings → Bring Your Own Carrier

  • Choose to edit the Voice Termination settings

  • Specify your provider’s name

  • Set signaling IP address

  • Enter desired SIP Port or leave it blank if you are planning to use the default one

  • If you are an advanced user, you may want to set authentication parameters and additional headers, using the Advanced settings.

  • Make sure to Submit your settings, once done.

You can also reset your voice termination settings to default values, by clicking on the Reset link on the bring your own carrier page. You will be then prompted to confirm the reset.

2.2. Setting up your carrier for SMS traffic

SMS Origination

Restcomm’s Programmable SMS product enables you to initiate SMS traffic through the SMPP Protocol from your existing PSTN Carrier infrastructure towards Restcomm Cloud and use RCML and/or the REST APIs to create advanced SMS applications.

To setup SMS origination with your own carrier for your Organization, please follow the steps below:

  • Navigate back to Your profile → Settings → Bring Your Own Carrier

  • Choose to configure the SMS Origination settings

  • Specify your provider’s name

  • Set your provider’s IP address

  • Enter the Port provided by your carrier

  • Select the SMPP version of you carrier

  • Set a Systemid and password used to bind

  • Check the box below if you want to use the same provider for both SMS origination and termination.

  • Submit your settings.

SMS termination

Restcomm’s Programmable SMS product enables your advanced voice applications to initiate SMS traffic from the Restcomm Cloud towards your existing PSTN Carrier infrastructure through the SMPP Protocol using RCML and/or the REST APIs.

To setup SMS Termination with your own carrier for your Organization, please follow the steps below

  • To configure your SMS termination provider, go to your profile → Settings → Bring Your Own Carrier

  • Choose to configure the SMS Termination settings

  • Specify your provider’s name

  • Set your provider’s IP address

  • Enter the Port provided by your carrier

  • Select the SMPP version of you carrier

  • Set a Systemid and password used to bind

  • Check the box below if you want to use the same provider for both SMS origination and termination

  • Submit your settings

You may want to reset your SMS origination settings to default values, by clicking on the Reset link next to them on the bring your own carrier page. You will be then prompted to confirm the reset.
